I remember the first day I met Kyle. We had been exchanging e-mails about him coming in to volunteer with Lambda and the Resource Office. I was sitting at the desk when this handsome blonde fellow walks in the door and introduces himself. I always thought of my volunteers as new friends and not just volunteers, but I had no idea that Kyle would become one of my kindest and dearest friends. He really is someone special.

If there's one thing I can say about Kyle, it would be that he's one super Democrat! I mean, I feel alittle blue every election day, but this guys has got a blue on the brain 365 days a year! He even founded his own GLBT Democrat organization at UGA, the Stonewall Students (which was even featured in The Advocate). He has so much drive and ambition, it makes me naucious. I know Kyle can handle it, though. He's fucking hardcore!

I have had so much fun with Kyle of the last two years. He's a staple at Boneshakers and he makes going to that hole-in-the-wall worth it. He's also alot of fun at parties and he mixes the best drinks. He's also a snappy dresser, cigar afficionado, and an all around great guy. I mean, there's not much you can harp on this guy for, he's just too good (unless you count his addiction to Aqua Linda, but who can resist a good burrito??)

The sad thing about leaving Georgia is saying good-bye to friends like Kyle. On the upside, I do look forward to coming down for every Atlanta Pride and partying with him in his stylish Midtown apartment. At least, with a fabulous friend like Kyle, I know where the party's at.

Since Kyle is probably the most fabulous gay man I've come across in years, he should get the "queerest of the queer" garbage tracks...
