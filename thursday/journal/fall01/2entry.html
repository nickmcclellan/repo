Second Entry: My first week at UGA

There's a first time for everything--and in this case, a second.

Academically, I'm so charged here! From all my hard work in high school, I got 24 hours of credit. That gives me a major edge over my peers. I'm going to really try to unleash the sleeping Mary Anne Gragg in me. It would be very cool if I graduate a Presidential Scholar...but I really doubt it. I'll just have to do my best and not the bare minimum. I really tended to do that in high school.

A short entry, granted. Coming to UGA, I felt like I could really flourish academically. Socially, I was unsure where I'd fit in, but I knew that I'd have all my other shit in order. It was a comfort, to say the least.

I'm meeting this gay guy Greg today. I'm sorta nervous, but this is a big step for me. Though its in my head, I'm thring to push back that I would possibly date him. I think it would be cool to have another gay buddy. And then we could branch out. We could be the first freshman gay clique. I really hope he likes me. I read on this gay resource site that after coming out, it's natural for gay guys to want to meet other gay guys...then to have a building sense of pride. I think this is a good thing for me. Greg could help me go to that Lambda group...I'm getting really ahead of myself. I'll try to reflect later on our meeting.

Yah, meeting Greg was a big deal for me. I'd never meet any other gay guy (except for one, and that ended badly). It was very nerve-racking for me...sitting in the Russell lobby waiting for him. I was afraid no one would want to be my friend.

I remember sitting in my room in Russell--likely on my computer--looking out of my door and seeing people walking by. They had already made friends, and I was sitting at home alone. It was depressing! I thought my life would be holed up in that suffocating space. Thankfully, that didn't last.
