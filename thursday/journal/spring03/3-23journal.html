It took forever to find a spot. Yuck. Anyways, I've decided to title this week "Deadwood" for a couple of reasons. For one, I've been listening to that song alot. It's a garbage b-side, in fact, it's the first garbage b-side I really ever heard. Aside from #1 Crush or any of garbage's other soundtrack singles that never appeared on albums, "Deadwood" was the first song I heard off of an imported garbage single. It was the single for "I Think I'm Paranoid." I got it at Media Play near my Target, and it was just about the time that I really got into garbage and was so amazed at the concept of b-sides, or, as I understood at the time, "unreleased tracks." "Deadwood" was interesting, it was new. I was bound to appreciate it, being garbage. I don't think I understood it for a while though, it really never meant anything of significance to me until I got over the whole Greg thing. That's nothing now, it was nothing then, it was that I just listened to "Deadwood", it made me feel sad that I couldn't have been a more positive influence on him. Plus, I really didn't give a shit about him anymore.

Well, this week is "Deadwood." It's just dark and simple like that I guess. I don't want to go into it too much, I just wanted to explain briefly the significance of "Deadwood." Excellent song (though I hear garbage dislikes playing it live), download it!
