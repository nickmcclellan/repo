Thursday, February 24, 2005

Darkest Hour

I know I've been worthless on the Marilia front. I guess I got wrapped up in other things, but I hope to accomplish a good send off by the weekend. I haven't forgotten.

For one, on Monday, Genevieve called me and asked if I wanted to run one mile at Ramsey. I said yes, because honestly, one mile at Ramsey is rather pleasant, and I think we could all use a good mile in each one of our days. Well, through some bizarre twist of events, we both ended up forgetting our ID cards, and Genevieve proposed we run our classic 4 mile circuit around campus and downtown Athens.

I can't believe she manipulated me into running 4 miles! I felt so used as I was bargaining with her, trying to make her commit to something that wouldn't make me feel like a fool. Well, I got her agree to running at my pace as long as we did the full 4 miles. That worked for me, I knew I could finish the circuit, but I didn't want her running off and leaving me if I decided to walk at some point.

Well, both she and I were surprised to see that I ran a fairly brisk pace the entire 4 miles, and I even managed to beat her in a sprint up the very grueling Lumpkin hill by the Episcopal Center. I think I may try to run this circuit three times a week. I really want to lose some body fat, and torturing myself seems to be the only sure fire way to do that. I have done it twice already this week. We did it yesterday, and it was even easier than Monday! Bring on the 5K, I know I can take it now!

In other better news, I will be going to see garbage play at the Tabernacle on April 14th in Atlanta. I got an e-mail newsletter talking about a garbage.com members only presale opportunity, so I joined the stupid fan club and bought the tickets this morning. My sister is the only one who said she would definitely go with me. She and I went to see garbage at Music Midtown my freshman year together, and that was pretty cool. I went to the whole festival with her. At one point, she even got me to the mosh pit for Incubus, which was kind of scary. That was the last time I saw garbage live.

But, let's face it, a live performance at Music Midtown is just about the most worthless thing ever! They didn't even headline, and you know they were just playing their older hits to an indifferent crowd who probably didn't even know who they were. garbage at the Tabernacle is going to be so much better! I am dying to hear a b-side performed live. I can't wait until April now. That's going to be the best week ever because their CD will be released that Tuesday. You can expect a Blog-o-licious week then, I can assure you.

I have a paper to write tonight that I've been putting off until the last minute. There must be a cure for Senioritus. Someone tell me what it is, please!

Monday, February 21, 2005

I'm Only Happy When It Rains

So, what did everyone think of that intense storm a few hours ago? Wow, you don't see one like that every day. I wanted to get an entry in before the storm, but I guess things just happened so fast, I had to get out there.

I enjoy watching thunderstorms. When I was younger, and we lived in Davie, FL, I used to sit out on the porch and watch the thunderstorms roll in, which occurred pretty frequently in South Florida. It was especially nice because Florida is so much flatter than Georgia, and there aren't nearly as many trees, so you could see the whole horizon. It would be nice to do that again, some day.

Sometime tomorrow I'm going to have an entry for Marilia. She's going away for 10 months, and I'm really going to miss her. There's going to be a link to her new Friends page too.

Last night, we went out to dinner with her, and I got her a cake. I had them write "Good Riddance Marilia," and we all had a good laugh. After dinner, we had an L Word viewing party. It went off better than I thought it would. It was even better than last year, and I was a director then. You think I'd have more street cred then, but what can you do? I might do it next week because those girls loved their lesbians so much. I shouldn't deny them the opportunity to watch softcore porn with decent plot on Showtime.

As for anything else, I'm holding together. I have a feeling things will be getting worse very soon. I'm just taking it easy, you know, until that happens.

Adios
