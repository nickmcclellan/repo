All We Ever Have Is Now

How does one say goodbye to someone like Padmini? It's not something I found very easy. I think the only way I got through it at all is by not even believing it. I'm going to see her next week, I know it! But I won't. I won't see her for a long while coming.

From the moment I met her, I knew she was special. Something's in the name Padmini that screams, "Remember me!" As the legend goes, I did. Me and Padmini—or The Pad, as she was known as in those days—became fast friends.

As wonderful as Junior year was for me, I found that I had really drifted away from people I didn't mean to. Padmini was one of them. Maybe it was the excess of roaches in her apartment or the fact that she only had one Sunday potluck, but I just didn't see her that much. That didn't mean I didn't see her ever, though. Nope, it was I who was there to witness her down her first pint of beer on her 21st Birthday: the stout Guiness, which she didn't end up finishing. And I did get the chance to see her at D.C. Pride. She had such a posh pad and the rats were all sorts of enormous (good thing they were only outside).

This year Padmini has been one of my best friends. Like some of my other awesome peoples, she really enriched my last two semesters at UGA. Though I may be jealous of her superior English-major brain, I adore her attendence of all the lesser known sausage festivals along with the drunk phone conversations that inevitably follow. Watching Showgirls for the first time would not have had the same kick if our drunk little Padmini wasn't chiming in with comments and outrageous outbursts.

And let's not forget her fabulous taste in movies that do not star Elizabeth Burkley. She showed me La Mala Educación, The Aviator, and that wierd movie with the lady who did abortions and liked to drink tea. This summer has been just keen because of Padmini's constant demand for delicious varieties of ice cream (and, by the way, you really knocked it out of the park with Graham Slam, girl). Oh, urr, or was it me who forced ice cream down our throats :-/

So yah, I can't say goodbye to movie nights, wine nights, crazy photographs at Jerm's apartment—it's not possible for me. I feel like I'm going to see her very soon and I'm just going to go with that feeling. I know I'm going to see her tomorrow.

I just know it.
