"Thursday"

Sunday, April 24, 2005

Old Friends

Before I start plugging away on the last paper of my undergraduate experience here at UGA, I just want to go into what happened Saturday. It was a truly special day.

Well, I woke up alittle later than I had hoped. I wanted to get out the door running by 9 or 10 a.m., but that turned out to be more around 10:20. I find it really hard to start running in the morning, especially when it's the first thing I do all day. Who's to say I have the physical strength to eclipse six miles that early in the morning? Well, eventually I started going at it.

It was an uneventful run. Disappointing in only one sense: lately, I have been allowing myself to walk. No, not like I used to walk a substantial portion of the circuit when I had run in the past, nothing like that. I just stop somewhere briefly, for maybe 50 paces, and walk. It's always a let down for me. I wish I had the strength to run the whole thing every time.

Someone did yell out their car at me. I don't even know what they said, but I was surprised how much it didn't bother me. I noticed that people do stupid stuff like that when I started riding my bike home from school. Some people can't help but to honk and yell at you to humiliate you. I don't know why anyone would do something like that. I thought about it alot, then finished my run with an extra sprint to my door to make up for those 50 paces.

After that, I was off to Marietta as fast as I could to see Nicole. She was having a cookout, like I mentioned, and I wanted to get there early so I could have some one-on-one time with her. I really do consider her one of my best friends, and I wanted a chance to connect with her personally. It was probably stupid, but it turned out well.

I went straight to her house and as soon as I saw her, I gave her a big hug. I shook her brother Conrad's hand. He'd definitely beefed up since the last time I saw him. Nicole's parents were really funny. They kept asking how I had been and I told them I'd be graduating in a three weeks. They'd get all suggestive to Nicole at that point, saying to me, "Wow, graduating in four years, that really great!" They enjoy giving their daughter a hard time about her choice to go to a law school in France, but she's sure she made the right decision. It's certainly made her happy, that's for sure.

Nicole and I ducked out long enough to take a stroll through he McEachern campus. It hasn't changed much. There's many more vending machines and recycling bins, which is both good and bad. It looks alot smaller, though. I guess Nicole and I, at some point, outgrew this place. It can't hold us anymore.

We went back for the food part of the cookout, and April showed up. I insisted on veggie burgers, and the Levesque clan suddenly started a vein of conversation on vegetarianism, which didn't turn out so bad. I think alot of people liked the veggie burgers. Nicole's grandmother kept asking what they were made out of.

After the family fun, we decided to go out and get a drink. We went down another walk down memory lane—at least for me. It was the first time I had been to my old Taco Mac since I lasted worked there. No one recognized me at all, which was alright. We had a very pricey pitcher of margaritas and Nicole had a Sweet Georgia Brown. I promised her when I went to see her in Angers this summer I'd try beer. For the first time.

April had a Mudslide which really turned out to be a fancy name for an alcoholic milkshake. Interesting concept—Nicole even recalled the days back at McEachern when I used to dip my french fries in my milkshakes at school. Even I had forgot I started that strange habit with her. Anyways, we didn't dip french fries in April's mudslide. We got alittle smashed, praised our server, and moved the party back to my house.

Jen got us some spirits and we continued there. Nicole got really drunk and frisky. There was some nominal kissing. I only mention this because I know Nicole's boyfriend Alain doesn't know a lick of English. She was tired, so April took her home. I gave her a big hug, told her I loved her, and that I'd be coming to see her very soon. Far, far away from here.

I didn't want to stay in Marietta, so I had numerous glasses of water to wash the Hurricanes and Amaretto Sours from my system. I had so much water that before I left my sister's house I think I peed like 3 times. I even had to stop at the QuikTrip on 316 to use the bathroom, I was about the explode. I never stop to use the bathroom. And I always thought I had the strongest of bladders.

One freak occurrence though, I was wearily cruising at 75 mph about to get on the loop when a cop ahead of me in the medium flashed his lights on quickly and turned them off. It shocked me completely awake. I got down to 55 mph so fast, I don't think he had the heart to pull me over. I thought I was a goner for sure. I arrived home safely, so don't worry.

So that was Saturday. Now it's Sunday and I have so many things to finish. Maybe at the end of this week, things will be different. We will certainly see.

In my happy home I barely breathe
I never once in my wayward life was heading to run out
