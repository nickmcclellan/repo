By:  Nick McClellan

 

Inside my head

A ribbon marks where I stand.

I’ve dragged it along

Through summer nights

outstretched, a ribbon

to catch me,

to conceal and remember

It’s here with me now.

 

It says you’re like them, the others

Like Jade, a pretty stone

sleeping in mud

sunken in mind with mysteries

wavy lines

Or like my precious feline

still lost among the stars

smiling, the world inside

to suspend

 

How I wish, for once

to lose my self, the stars

the guiding lights exposing

mockeries of reason

to sink as well

to fall to feel

something before the ground.

 

They were the last

it said—the ribbon in my hands

I never cried for them

I simply stripped away

Every stone, every piece of myself

Built my world around

the hovel into which I’ve crawled

 

Tore it down

my self

And made dirty

Ugly things!

my self

And I became them

 

I weighted an affirmation that never came

Learning to live among those doubts

I thought you’d wash away

I thought you’d do something.

But, cradled in ribbon

They remained

 

 

Shamefully awake, pressured,

light headed,

An hour ago

I writhe in ignored agony

In a place I’ve been before

Cooling in your shadow.

 

I heard everything

everything you knew

how it felt

To give me what I wanted

to know I cared.

 

You’d say I’m pathetic

And hollow

and my words are like snakes

Sent to entrap you

my words become lies

the noose around your neck

I cringe when you say it chokes you.

 

You stopped.  I turned away

Once again

I clung to ravaged cotton

And ribbon…

time was all I had.

 

I opened my eyes

To see that familiar glow of gloom

a shadow—impressions

inside, it’s darker

barren and cold.

 

and all I could feel

Was my ribbon

and this new scar

above the others

Dark and deserved

under my coarse blanket

pressed by my crossed arms

my head lowered.

 

And all I could feel

pockets of jade

the whine of a feline

these shadows and lies

impressions and deception

how it feels so real

inside my head.
