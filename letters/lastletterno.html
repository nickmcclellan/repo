"How is your new life?"

I got this picture from a photographer at the Times-Delta. This is the Sierra Nevada Mountains viewed from Visalia on a clear day. Breathtaking—first time I saw them I almost drove off the road.
...

I was a bit taken a back when Marilia's mother asked me that. She probably meant to say, "How is your new job?" but it must have come out wrong. But, then I thought about it. I do have a new life, don't I? It's very different.

I have been living in Visalia for about a month now. I have my own place. I have a full-time job. I even have furniture. I think the thing I can boast about the most would be my rice cooker. I absolutely love that!

My first few weeks in Visalia were hectic, but very interesting. I did end up getting the nice 2 bedroom, 1 bath I was gunning for. After pulling all of my stuff out of my car that Saturday, I drove around for hours looking for a futon to sleep on (at least until I had enough money to buy a bed and a mattress). It was an eventful day—some lady swung her door open when I was pulling into my Target parking space and I hit her door. Not even 48 hours and I'm involved in an accident. Great.

Work started on Halloween. That was freaky, considering a bunch of my fellow employees had dressed up for the occasion. The first person I saw when I walked in the door happened to be Frankenstein, prompting me to wonder if I had made the right decision coming all this way to work at a monster mash. Despite getting the creeps, I was scared that I would be overwhelmed and the paper would be disappointed with my level of experience. That wasn't the case. I didn't even design any pages the first day. When I finally did do some, I found I really did have a lot to offer.

This is a tree that grows in the middle of the road by my apartment. I run by it almost every day. I just think it's kind of cool.
I have my own desk, phone and computer at work. If you can believe it, I have my own business telephone number! It's pretty cool. The coolest thing about my job is the fact that I have a brand new G5 Mac computer! It looks like a hummer, and I'm the only copy editor that has one! It's pretty nifty, even my bosses don't have one like mine.

Most of the time I've spent here has been preoccupied with collecting furniture for my apartment. While I had some stuff left over from Athens, as far as necessary things like a bed and dining room table, I had nada. The first place I went was, of course, Ikea. After my first week at the Visalia Times-Delta, I went down to Los Angeles to see my friend Melissa who I went to high school with. She went with me to Burbank and went down to Hollywood Boulevard, where I was frightened by all these people dressed up as celebrities who tried to accost me. Once again, freaked out in California, but I survived.

A few weeks after that I got to see San Francisco for the first time. I went to visit my friend Amber, who goes to Stanford in Palo Alto. We took the Caltrain down into the city where I got flirted with at both the Safeway and the Ghiradelli shop. It was pretty neat, so many gay people! I can't wait to go party with some of my other gay friends. I also visited the Palo Alto Ikea to satisfy my new Ikea addiction (at long last netting myself a dining room table).

As far as life in Visalia, it's pretty sweet. I have money now! I can pay for groceries and I don't have to live off my credit card anymore. Every morning, I wake up at around 9 or 10, bring in my New York Times (when they decide to deliver it) and put on my gym shorts and shoes. I go out and run about 4 miles and come back and do my push-ups and crunches before taking a shower. After that, I read my newspaper over breakfast, which is sometimes eggs and toast, but mostly just my tradition post-run meal of peanut butter sandwiches. After that, I usually run some errands, whatever it is I have to do before work at 2:30.

The Visalia Times-Delta is one of the oldest newspapers in the Valley. Maybe that's why the building looks like it was made in the 19th century.
For dinner, I come home and make any one of the many meals I've become accustomed to making in my kitchen. Mostly tofu. Since I've moved into my own place where I can make as much of a mess as I want, I find I make tofu about 3 times a week. I've got three blocks in the fridge right now! Sometimes I make it with pasta. Sometimes I have it with rice. That's probably my most favorite meal. I make it with rice and veggies (which I can cook together with my nifty new rice cooker) and it's absolutely delicious.

So yes, I am enjoying my new life. This weekend I'm going out with this guy and Fresno, so chalk another California town to my list. That's probably the only thing that I really miss the most: friends. Though I call a whole heck of a lot of people back home, it would be nice to have people I can hang out with while I'm tooling around in Visalia. I'm sure I'll be assimilating into the area fairly soon.

Well, that's about all I can say about Visalia. I hope you liked my letters. This will be the last one, but if I feel like chiming in with an update before I launch my new Web site next summer, this is probably where I'll do it.

Even though I may be spirited away, you can always find me. I'm pretty easy to track down.

Love you guys. Later.

Oh, and this is me—riding on the Caltrain after a long day strutting by the Bay. I may be several hundreds of miles away, but it's still me.
