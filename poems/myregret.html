On a February evening, in a subway train,
My choices have disarmed me.
I stand alone—the one who listens—
While a cold breeze enters the car.
The woman beside me shivers against her partner;
He smiles, pressing her closer.
The man to my left prods his elbow into my side
Moving his newspaper away from the shifting air.
The cold brings tears to my squinting eyes
I wipe them away onto my shoulder with my coat.
My hand clinches the arm holster above me
And I stare out into the window ahead.
My translucent reflection appears to soar through the city
Its lights cascade across my face
The wind outside races by against my window
Rushing a blast of flurries past.
The subdued woman before me looks half-alive
Her reflected eyes seeming splintered and broken—
A part pressed in this train,
and part crouching at your riverside.
I see the people bustle beside my reflected self
And I imagine you are standing here beside me,
your hair wafting in the cold air
Northbound, on a Friday night.

On the mudbank of a sunken river
In the heart of a silent forest
I wait for you.
At the edge of the water
Kneeling in the fallen dust of your final steps
I watch the sun’s dying light
Laid upon the ripples you made in the water.
My palms wipe the tears from my eyes
and brush the dry mud from my skirt.
I scream out to you, pleading that you’d regret
pouring out my tears for you to live.
I know your soft dying eyes can see me
writhing in the violent dusk.

You think I'm crazy.
I'm just some stupid girl
Lost at the edge of the water
Wrestling with things I cannot change
my shoes dug into the mud
While you sink beneath the surface.
Rays of milky light drift down and graze your cheek
You can barely hear me in the echoing depth
As your ears fill with water and your ear drums burst.

You think that I don’t understand
How pointless it was to love her
though you earnestly tried.
You think I’ll never respect
The innocent seduction on that wobbling canoe;
your shaking hand against her pleated blouse.
And you think I could never fathom or comprehend
The bitterness of her betrayal
and the edge of her wounding arrow.
I’ll never hear those hateful whispers
she spit into your ear
As she curled her legs around your thigh
and promised you love in death.
I am just a useless girl, of course
mourning you in mud,
ignorant and insensitive to the past.

I can hardly see you now
Dancing with her beneath the water
Her brittle hands holding down your head
Your hair frozen between her dead fingers
As your image fades into reflected moonlight.
I let out a rough sigh
Clinching my fists in the dirt
Whimpering as the last tear falls past my ear.
I lie there for hours after the water calms
My eyes soften and the tears dry
The water bubbling softly beneath my exhaled breath.

I remember hearing your muted cries
as you laid stricken on my bedroom floor.
Glancing at me through your wild, weary eyes
Your shirt drenched in dried blood, pressed against your wound.
I shook anxiously at your side,
Petrified at what I presumed to feel
Your head resting in the folds of my skirt—
the starlight shone on your sleeping face,
My fingers roaming through your smooth black hair—
I should have known I was too stupid to love you.

On that cramped subway train,
Staring into the glass in front of me
I am so close to the riverbank:
Standing at the edge of the water
Leaning toward the depth
But I do not fall.
My heart is still beating in my chest
And I will not drown in the arms of my lover.
I thought you would come for me
But I thought foolishly then
with the unbroken heart of a girl.

This was not circumstance—a sudden ebb in the river.
I am no longer the stupid girl at the edge of the water,
Praying to the mysterious, shifting sky.
I am the one who stares blankly into a cold glass,
Living despite your wretched death,
Pretending I am real.

This was your will,
your fevered glance—

And this is my regret.
