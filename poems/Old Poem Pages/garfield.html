By:  Garbage

 

        Trapped within the pages of a book, I’m dying to escape, to leave this prison.  The nightmare ends tomorrow, but do sweet dreams follow?  What promises the end of the darkness, the solitude, the abyss?  Is it a higher power?  Is it our own foolish short-sighted hope?

        Throwing my books on the floor, I dream of that day.  I can leave all my miseries behind in this hovel of despair.  I can leave my room empty and hollow, one less person it has swallowed.  I dream of that day I can lay on my bed and let my troubles spin circles with the blades of the fan that spins overhead.  My troubles stuffed beneath the sticky-tak of my assorted posters.  I dream of the night I can fall asleep and return to my other life, the one before this one.

        Peering through the blinds, Garfield gives the situation a look see.  Suction cupped to the window, Garfield asks, “So why are you running away?”

        Rolling my head on my pillow, I answer, “Because things got complicated, things got old.  Things aren’t the way they used to be…”

        Garfield, with a sly grin, adds, “Well isn’t that what you expected from college in the first place?”

        I pause, remembering that hope I help deep inside when I left my home to try a different place.  Remembering the power and prestige such a new place could grant me.  “I guess so, but so what.  Things will be different next year.  Next year, I’ll know the difference between the good and the bad.  Next year…”

        “…Is just going to be as complicated and familiar as this year, nothing changes.” Garfield interrupts.  He left paw slips off the window, and I push the suction cup firmly back in place.

        “You don’t understand, you weren’t there for all of it.  The nights I spent searching for someone I could love, the nights I would return and feel that empty pit inside just swallow another piece of my heart.  You didn’t see the struggle.  There’s something out there, there has to be.  There’s something there for me…”

        “I think you are just lying to yourself.  You’re a boy in a man’s world.  There are things out there that can hurt you more then you can imagine.  Perched silent on your window sill, I can see more then you think.  While you sleep soundly, they gather, wanting your pure innocence so they can corrupt it and create guilty pleasures so they can fill their emptiness.  You must beware of these monsters…”

        Sigh…I have plush novelty comic characters discussing my inner turmoil, how can I explain this?  “I know they’re there.  I can see them, how their lips wrap loosely around their bleach white fangs, I can see this.  I know of the emptiness that envelops their tortured, dying souls.  I know the danger, but I know the hope.  The hero has a name, and he’s waiting for me…”

        1:23.  “It’s late, I need to sleep.”

        “Don’t forget.  It takes courage to face those demons.  It takes courage to know the difference between the fake and the fantastic.  If you want to be saved, it’s going to take more courage and patience then you can stand…”  The doll trails off until his plush lips seal and his wide eyes harden and look away.

        Wrapping myself in sheets, I wince at the glimmer of street light shining onto my face.  Turning over, I close my eyes, waiting to accept the new day.

        “It takes courage to know the difference between the fake and the fantastic.  It takes humility to realize when you have made a mistake and you must learn from it.  It takes failure to realize you’re wrong.  And it takes duplicity to run from these failures…but courage can change them.  You’re the hero if you can face these.  You must save yourself to be saved; no hero will do it for you.  The hero is the enduring strength inside that grants you the sanity to love another helpless runaway…”  Garfield’s wily smile fades as he falls defeated off the window into the darkness…
