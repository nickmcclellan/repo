soft, and soothing
the field longs for touch
cold, and heavy
the air drips down my throat

the mountain towers in the west
but I'm not looking back
I've left you smiling at the top
and weathered the downward trail

Through light, and dark, and beaten hope
through long and frigid nights
I've struggled through your false conception
and fallen placid here

The arrow never crossed my eyes

Slamming hard into my chest
The blood bursts from the sanded wound
Tears and blood trickle down my heaving chest
Mixing into globes of earthy sky
Underlined by the sanguine beast

The field whips slowly in the fluid of your breath
The soft hum of your voice brushes past my ear
I smile, sinking into my ruddy pool
I lose myself in the notes of your sirens
and look up the weathered trail

I see your wily, and selfish face
Hands playing with your bow
I rise to break my blood chains
forcing the arrow to the ground
I gather my composure that was taken by your thrust

I continue down the field
