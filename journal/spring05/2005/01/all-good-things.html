Tuesday, January 25, 2005

All good things...

I feel like I've been hiding lately. I mean, I'm not opposed to such a notion. I feel like hiding. I think I hide someplace new every semester. Last semester, it was in the SLC. Have you ever slept in the chairs in the SLC on the fourth floor? I have. It's actually quite nice. I remember, last year before we got the SOAR award, I had spent the previous night doing a paper, and I was sleeping up there before I went down to accept the award. It's quite comfortable.

Well, the big news is that I have decided to convert to vegetarianism. It's not quite as big as it sounds, though. I've always said it was something I saw myself doing one day. I guess it was Genevieve who really turned me onto it—though a few others gave me a nudge or two. For one, I heard that when you stop eating meat, it's like you feel alot healthier. I'm not sure if that's true or not, but I certainly feel different. Also, I really hate to prepare meat, I have never been very into that. It's so disgusting. Honestly, it really hasn't been that difficult to convert. I've been eating lots of fruits and veggies, and I tend to prepare more meals at home. It's been a really positive thing, so far.

Also, this week, our heater is on the fritz. I spent Saturday and Sunday night in a very deep freeze. I stacked blanket upon blanket to keep me from waking up with the chills. My roommates were obviously upset, but I was a bit cooler about the whole situation. I called the landlord on Sunday and he was nice enough to get the heat guy out that afternoon. The heater worked for a few hours before going kaput again. At this point, Jennifer was screaming for blood (at about 10 p.m.), but I told her calling the landlord and waking him up wasn't going to do any good. Last night, I figured out how to get the heat to work at least for short intervals. Luckily, it carried on through the night before going out this afternoon. I hope this isn't permanent.

I want to go the the Canterbury Tales thing tomorrow night at Tate! Does anyone want to go? I mean, if you do, all you have to do is use that nifty comments feature that I changed my whole frickin' Blog to accommodate. I don't mind an anonymous post now and then. I can always delete them of course :-D

I'll close with another peak at a Friends Page. I'm especially proud of Genevieve's page. So check it out.

Until next time.
