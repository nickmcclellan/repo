Do What You Want

It hasn't been a good day. For a while now, I have been having a hard time accepting frequent disappointment. Not from whom you'd expect, I should add, but just from people who I care for and you would think care as equally for me. I feel like I'm a good person, that I am someone who listens and tries very earnestly to make happy the people I care about. This week, for example, I sent packages to Marilia and April (of the pervasive Peace Corps fame) at significant expense to me. I mean, I'm not even sure if they would have gone such lengths for me, but I didn't do it for that. I don't expect something in return. I know they deserve something special and I did everything I could to make them happy—even if it cost too much.

I don't think anyone would do something that special for me—or anything special for me. I even make myself very clear to the people that are capable of doing the most trivial things to give me the smallest amount of satisfaction, and I am ignored. Instead of listening to me, though, they would rather think about themselves and, as a consequence, make me feel worthless. Shouldn't my graduation be special? Don't I deserve for my most simple requests to be fulfilled? Why must I try so hard for others when no one will try for me? I know it's not much, and though they seem trivial, the fact that you ignore them hurts me very much. I don't know why I should just accept that nobody gives a damn about me. It's not fair, and I won't take it anymore.

So no, I don't care that I'm graduating tomorrow. I don't care what I'm wearing, I could give a shit which tassel graces my cap. It doesn't matter to me because it obviously doesn't matter to anyone else. I believe that if it did matter, you would do something about it and I wouldn't feel like a burden and a fool. You would listen to me instead of treating me like shit.

Anyways, I told myself I would write something before the big day. I don't think I will even show up, honestly. Who's going to notice anyways? I never said I wanted to go. It's a pointless ceremony where you sit around and listen to a bunch of boring speeches until someone hands you a piece of paper that says you graduated, which you already know, and I just don't see the point in it all. Can you believe I was asked to go to more than one of these things? I can't handle this. I just want it to be over.

And the only thing I wanted to do more than anything else today was to run. And I didn't even get to do that.

So happy graduation my fellow students. You earned it.
