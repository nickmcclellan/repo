Saturday, March 26, 2005

Out Firefoxed

It really was a beautiful day today. In fact, it's been nice ever since the dial changed from cruel winter to forgiving spring. When I went out to lunch with John and Ben on Friday, Ben took a look out the window and said, "This weather would put anyone in a good mood." That got me thinking. Am I in a good mood? Do I really prefer a sunny day to a severe thunderstorm?

I don't know. But I did take advantage of the day. I did some extra credit running with Genevieve on the 4 mile route. I also did some weights with Jerm and some swimming too. I feel all good now, and I even noticed I lost 3 pounds. So, in that respect—in the only thing I have any control over—I did have a good day. The weather was pleasant too.

As I mentioned yesterday, I got Firefox. I didn't give it much of a look, but after wrestling with my Blog's CSS yesterday, I was unable to give my Mozilla viewers the joy of a semi-transparent layout. I guess that's just what you get for using a browser that doesn't know how to make text opaque.

Yeah, so consider this my endorsement of a guarded Internet Explorer. Sure, it's got lots of holes, but that's only because they've got about every virus writer in the world gunning to take down the big one. Maybe if Mozilla could differentiate from transparent background and opaque text, it would be riddled with ruthless attacks as well. Hopefully those mousy Firefox programmers will give me my opaque text, and I'll join my fellow indie-browser elite. In the meantime, I'll be with good ol' E (but I will be reading the news on Firefox. That "open as tabs" option is really cool).

Well, I should go. All this running is bound to make me tired. Hope everyone has a chocotastic Easter. I've got my basket.

See you mister chicken.
