Friday, January 21, 2005

No Gain

I'm in alot of pain right now. Yes, it's considerable. On Wednesday, I had my normal load of four classes straight. Of course, I started my day in Gilbert Hall, and then I went to Fink in Journalism. This class rocks, of course, because Fink is a God and he knows all. After that, I had to trek on my bike all the way down to Ramsey for my very first day in lifting weights in beginners weight training. I was the weakling doing bicep curls with only the bar. After that I had to ride my battered body on my bike all the way back to North Campus (to Park Hall, to be exact) for my Women and Chaucer Class. I was listening to Lauryn Hill's "The Conquering Lion" when I was making the final approach up the hill, and it was rather ironic. When I got home that day, I felt like I wanted to fall over and die.

So I took a nap. It was delicious, as far as naps are concerned. I did some teacher training this week for CSS, and that turned out to be cookielicious. We played musical chairs, but I won, so that was good. It would have sucked to be the one in the middle that had to answer all the questions. I also bought a new backpack this week because the one I had couldn't hold my laptop. The one I've got now is all spacious, and computer friendly, which is nice.

Anyways, I'm still plugging away at those Friends Pages. After I'm finished with all that, I think I'll turn my attention to the Galleries Page, and then I'll start to write my semester Blogs. Not sure if I mentioned, but I want to do a special "looking back" Blog for each year I spent at UGA. They'll basically be set on the Archive page, featured with their respective semesters, so that people can get a bigger picture look at the experience without having to sift through all that crap. I may even go back and correct some HTML errors that are abounds, certainly. Oh, how I love endless updating...ahhh

So check out Leena's page. She's been liberated from Dille, as I mentioned. I'm still waiting on Dille's photos, maybe I'll work on her page tomorrow.

Anyways, I hope everyone has a nice weekend. I know my weekend will be carefree (Senioritus prevents me from really caring about homework...and there's nothing anyone can do about it!)

Monday, January 17, 2005

Repression

I'm going to make posting in my Blog the new fad for the semester. I updated my old school website yesterday announcing my departure from Geocities. It was a nice run, but I like the UGA server so much better. Ever since I got Studio MX 2004, I have been so website capable! I am able to edit a page, upload it, and view it in a matter of seconds, it's pretty amazing. FTP upload ability is dreamy, of course.

Blogger is ok, I have owe much thanks to this useful little tool. I really value having autonomy of my Blog's appearance and location. I was surprised that I could still have both w/ Blogger, so I guess that's why it's sticking around. Plus, I don't have to make a whole new webpage every week, all I do is write a post, and Blogger takes care of archiving and all that other messy stuff. Good times.

I'm updating my Friends Page very slowly. I did Tee and Quiana tonight, and I plan to get around to everyone before...well, before sometime, maybe next week? Classes are, well, they are there, I'm not stressing myself about them too much yet. I realize I'm going to have to get my ass in gear eventually, but I'm dealing with alot right now, it's nice now and then to get a weekend of laziness and repression. That would be this weekend, of course.

This week, I'm going to do my graduation check, and get my completion of minor for Spanish. We do teacher training for ESL this week, so I might go look into volunteering at the CSS office on Tuesdays or Thursdays in addition to the teaching. I'd like to do something for my Spanish, because I would really like that to help with the job search.

Well...I'll make an announcement on my recent New Year's Resolution in a few days. It's just a little unbelievable (I'll have to tell Diane first).

Ok, later peoples!!!

Sunday, January 16, 2005

Voices

Flashy new Blog. But I have nothing to say about it.

There's no overwhelming feeling of completion that has washed over me since I've started my last semester here at UGA. There's no echo of any past Spring semester that indicates just how this is all going to end. It's just me, and my problems. Me, and the hole I seem to have fallen into. The truth I have, until now, considered only as an impossible resort, is all that remains before me. I'm going to have to get myself out of here, and no one else is going to do it for me.

Ok, so that was lame, but it's true. The one thing I want to do more than anything is get out of here. I don't know where I want to go exactly, but I know where I don't want to be, and that's anywhere close to this place. Maybe it's Athens, or the South, or even the election that convinces me this place sucks. I feel like I've worked a full four years for something, and now it's time to receive it. I feel like I've watched so many friends go places, and now I want to go somewhere special, and I want to go now. I have no idea where it's going to be.

The other day, I got a digest from this group I'm in for copy editors. A job across the San Francisco Bay, open for a new graduate, experienced in copy design. Wouldn't that be great? California? I've never gone that far. Noa was telling me about this temporary work program in England over the summer, though I'm not sure if I'll do it. I know I want to go to Europe at some time this summer. I have yet to determine how.

I just have alot of plans, and time, but not an awfully lot of it. Maybe someone else is afraid, like I am, that graduation truly means the end of everything. I'm hopeful it's just the beginning.

In other news, I heard LiveJournal got shut down for an extended amount of time. That sucks for bloggers, it's nice I have all my schtuff on a server to provide un-interrupted Blog goodness. I guess, for a while there, the voices stopped. People had to actually talk to eachother to get the point across. Maybe there's hope for us all.

So, yah, a new year. Expect a graduation, maybe a descent Spring Break. Alot of angst and pain. And me. I'm in here, somewhere.
