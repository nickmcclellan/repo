Put a fork in it, I think it's done.

My computer may finally be dead and gone. It wouldn't start yesterday and I figured as soon as I plugged it into the wall it would start working again...but it didn't. I scrambled through my room looking for any way to fix my computer, I even bugged April about getting a screwdriver again so I could get inside and see if maybe a piece of dust had taken hostage my computer. I have a shit computer. I am trying to call my mom to see if she'd help me finance a new laptop, but we'll see. I was really upset last night, but this afternoon, I realized that it's not that big a deal. This is oddly reminiscent of my freshman year when my dorm phone AND my computer AND probably something else essential all broke at the SAME TIME. I remember jumping in my car to go to Target or something to get on fixing things...it was then I realized, looking at my shit-mobile, the one thing I can always trust to be unreliable, it was functioning fine and would help me get things done. I had a similar realization driving to the mall today, and I thank God that He decided to keep my Mazda trucking at least one more week before I lose my front axle. And you know how important THOSE are...


I talked to my mom and I'm a little more calm about the laptop thing. I feel so bad, she seems to bear the burden of my needs, my unnecessary wants. I think I need a computer. My dad may disagree. I think I need a car. My dad disagrees, he seems more willing to deny me these things where my mom wants to provide them for me, putting herself through hell to do it. She said she'd wait to move out of her mom's apartment to get me a car, and that made me sad. Why do I have to be such a burden on her? I feel better now, I've really been in stickier situations. Last year, my computer crashed when it got a virus (i.e. journal entry "Crashed"). That sucked, I remember going to dinner with Sonya and Missy going on and on about fixing my computer, I felt like I had to fix it that instant, it was agonizing. I was fine in the end. Patience is something I really should learn. As far as getting over comforts like my laptop, I think I'm making powerful strides, especially regarding my car. As far as making it to campus, I've been riding my bike and taking the bus. Today, I took the car cause I knew I had to go to Best Buy. I enjoy riding my bike, I'm going to have a toned ass like nobody's business.

And that's another thing! I looked in the mirror this morning, and maybe it was last night's stressing about my computer, but I look damn good. I've come along way from half-off all the food I can eat at Taco Mac.
