Is it Fall yet?

Monday, August 16th

It's started, right before my eyes, everything has been set in motion. I was thinking...this year is unlike all the others. In the past, I have had to endure an ambitious move before each Fall semester. My first year, it was a momentous occasion. Days after I moved into Russell, the realization that this was the first time in my life that I was alone was very powerful. After I moved into Mary Lyndon, I met so many new people, and began the most exciting and life-changing semester since I've been in college. Last year, when I moved into my house, I endured an exhaustive move all by myself from Marietta to Athens. That night, I set up my bed among all the strewn pieces of furniture in my room, curled up with Matt, and slept like a baby. Every year, it's been big and messy, and I've always been some place different. This year, however, is different.

Yesterday, I worked on the website in my new room. Since Marilia moved out in mid-June, my house has been in transition. David moved in, and I helped him. I got an influx of cups and plates, very much appreciated. The living room has taken on several transformations. I've moved into Milo's room. Certainly, things have been different, but they've been different for a long time. Now that Jennifer is living here, things are slowing down, but that by no means makes this August special. Nothing happened yesterday, and even though I didn't expect anything to happen, I'm still surprised.

Is it fall yet? Maybe not in August, but some day soon. But this week is important. Thursday, classes start again. Then Friday, they continue. And then weeks and weeks of more classes, parties, people, and change. All of it very important, and we all know how Fall has an effect on everyone. Yesterday, I couldn't help thinking about how many new people arrived yesterday and how they will have such a profound impact on my last year here. Whether they snicker when I ride by on my bike, hoot and holler while their drunk downtown, or if they're stopping by the Lambda Office to see what's going on. They all arrived yesterday, and to them, these days are the most important ever, but from my place here, at my house, I can't even see why.

Anyways, that's what I was thinking yesterday. I also did a lot of nothing at all. I worked alot on my webpage, fixed some bugs. It looks really good, I'm happy how it turned out. I miss the old theme, but it's nice to get a new one. Lately, I've been thinking what I'll do with the webpage when I stop updating next May. I want to recreate all the themes and have everyone who visits the front page to see randomly each theme. It would be the same basic links, but the front page will reflect a past theme each time upon refresh. That way, people will see every icon, every background. I know it's possible, but I suppose I'll worry more about that next Spring.

Today, once I get moving, I'm going to take my bike to get fixed. I'm going to be using it a bunch, I'm sure. Come to think about it, I need to check on those free parking passes. Can't trust Parking Services to just get off their ass and send you the passes like you'd asked of them. I miss the depiction of Parking Services as being a bunch of monkeys in the Red & Black cartoons. Those were always surprisingly accurate. Anyways, I better get a move on. More on my week later...and more new icons! Hurray :-)
