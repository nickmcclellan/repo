Saturday, October 2nd

The week is over, thankfully. It was the roughest week all year for me, certainly. As I mentioned Monday, I've been dealing with a whole bunch in the last month. September earns the title "Worst Month Ever" for several reasons.

Yah, the money issues have been bad. I think I spent my money on worthwhile things, though. My teeth are important, right?! I can eat carrots again! That reminds me, I should pick up some. Also, I feel good spending money on my car. I feel like I'm establishing a relationship with the bucket of pretty-lookin' bolts. It's money well spent, and I certainly need my car. Also, did anyone know I got in a little fender bender on Wednesday? Yah, it happened. It wasn't that bad though. Genevieve said there wasn't even a dent, so that's really not bad.

So today is the LSU game, which is hopefully my last extreme brush with what I like to call the "Bulldawg Nation" mentality. It's a school of though that encourages ignorance, intoxication, the massive consumption of fossil fuels with countless SUV's and RV's, and the mindless and unexplained worship of football. Never have I seen something so incredibly boring, especially during games involving those lovable Bulldawgs. We play, and we play, year in and year out. Our schedule is loaded with those classic (and at most times very easy-to-win) SEC rivals like Auburn and Tennessee. They also load us up with some wannabe-winners like Arkansas and Marshall (and just who the fuck is Marshall?!) And just when we need to win (aka last year's TWO games against LSU and the ill-fated Florida game), we always lose. It doesn't actually matter, though. Losing just means you buy the drinks when you go downtown afterward, and of course the ladies are much more sympathetic. Hopefully you're not one of those sissy faggots, though. Oh no, they have no place in a Bulldawg Nation. You might as well be bloodied-up and beaten on a sidewalk outside of Boneshakers before you ever consider yourself a citizen of this great Southern tradition. I think I will go out tonight, though. Maybe I will even return the ladies sympathy. But I'm not wearing red today. No, I'm wearing Black.

In other news, I thought the debate Thursday was so very entertaining. Just because Bush and his lackeys say Kerry can't lead a war on terror doesn't mean it's true. I think alot of people say the difference between fact and spin during those 90 minutes. It's a shame all of those Bush and Kerry supporters were outside with those raised placards that basically said, "Let me tell you what happened!" Like we couldn't figure it out ourselves. Round One: Kerry.
