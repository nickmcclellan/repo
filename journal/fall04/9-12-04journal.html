Thursday, September 16th
. . . Happy Anniversary Mom

So Hurricane Ivan finally decided to do something instead of churning out in the ocean. He cancelled my class, turned off all my TV's, and shut off my internet. Thanks Ivan, I needed that.

The class thing is fine with me, though. We had actually started, and then it was like God came on over the intercom and told us all to go home because of the inclement weather. It was great, Professor Teague was like, "Wait! I have to show you something on Galileo!" Well, we stayed for that, and then I walked to my car in the rain. An hour early, I was huddled in Memorial Hall with my new Lambda friend because of the tornado warning. My roommate tells me that it touched down by Milledge. I was sort of hoping it would blow the stadium away. I think it would be really interesting to see what happed to "Bulldawg Nation" if the capital city is taken off the map. Viva la Resistance!

Now I'm just sort of huddled in my dark room listening to Matt's songs. They're nice. I wanted to share what the last week has been like for me. Basically, the surgery has been the focus point of emotion for me. It was a breeze of course, it didn't hurt, it was just an annoyance, monetarily and logistically. I hated the gauze in my mouth. I hated how I was dizzy and could not remain conscious on the way home or even when I was waiting for my sister to get the damned apple sauce. Matt came over on Friday and took care of me. I think we slept alot, but by the evening I was walking around with my puffy face and we went to Barberitos to get a burrito. We watched Beetle Juice finally, and then he went off to work for the weekend.

My other weekend activities did not include work, but instead a whole new sinister selfish type of work. Basically, thanks to sweep of Maypril's friend's page, I came across an online thing Evan was involved in (see links above). This "FreeiPod.com" deal works like this: I participate in an "easily cancelable" offer online and goad 5 of my friends to do the same thing. I've combed over the terms many times, and I have to get 5 unique people to do it, so I had lined up Matt, Mom, Jen, Laura, and Genevieve. Well, I've since expanded my effort and have gotten 2 likely completed offers from other means. I even went onto some message boards and netted some people, but I'm still waiting to see if they complete offers. It's a pretty groovy plan they've got going. I know they make a good amount of money by just getting one person to sign up for the offer, and I'm doing all the footwork to get people to join, so it's a sweet deal for them, no catch (trust me, it's rough convincing your friend to sign up for a "free trial"). I've nonetheless been rather successful, and will keep everyone informed on the status of my iPod. I expect that by the end of the weekend I'll have 5 people and then they just have to verify the shipping address. It's just so cool if I can screw this company out of an iPod. The fun thing for me is that I am neither willing nor able to get an iPod myself, but I'll still get one! Trust me.

I just feel bad about asking people to do the whole "free trial" thing. But then again, I ask strangers to do this all the time at Dial America, and at least this way I can tell my friends how to get themselves out of these deals. I don't want people to think it's a scam. This is my job, people. I know what I'm doing. Trilegiant taught me well (those slimy bastards).

Anyways, I'm going to save my battery strength for another hour. I think I'll go through a ball at a wall, or I'll go watch the storm. Looting would be fun. Here's to looting!
