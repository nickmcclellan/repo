Wednesday, October 20th

I haven't heard The Smiths "Asleep" for a while. I'm listening to it now, and it's very soothing. So much more haunting than "I Know It's Over," which, in my opinion, is just too loaded. As far as sleep anthems go, my personal favorites are garbage's "Sleep", "You Look So Fine", and "So Like A Rose." "Sleep" for obvious reasons, and "You Look So Fine", probably because the first time I really listened to that song, I was sitting in my car waiting for Jennifer to come in from Kroger. We both used to work there, and she was probably getting her paycheck or something. It was raining, and I just remember that whole line about, "Let's pretend / Happy end" playing over and over again while there was a light drizzle on my wind shield. It was very relaxing.

I just like "So Like A Rose," and any song that comes on at the end of a garbage album is usually a sleep anthem. "Milk" not so much, but anyways, I don't know how I got onto this train of thought. I guess I'd like to write about music today. I think I'd like to share with you the songs that I like and what they mean to me. So, here goes:

Ayumi Hamasaki - Dearest

 This is a very special song from the end credits of Inuyasha. It was actually the end credits that started at the end of the episode when Kanna reverses the wind scare, severely injuring Inuyasha. It was revealed that Kikyo was responsible for giving Naraku the power needed to hurt Inuyasha like this, and the tone at the end of the episode was that of unexplained betrayal. Why did Kikyo do something like that to Inuyasha? "Dearest" begins in much the same way the episode ends, with each character reflecting on the current state of things.

This song makes me think of Matt. It's very sweet, yet sad, though overall comforting. Ayumi has a beautiful voice, and she really captures the emotion of the moment, at least in the episode. Why I think of Matt, I don't really know. I guess the song's just innocent enough to do the guy justice, that's all. It's a testament to the good guys, when you feel like you've got nothing left, but one more thing.
New Order - Bizarre Love Triangle

 I was actually told to download this song from a friend that I knew who lived in California. I think I was listening to "Blue Monday" at the time, and he told me that I should check this song out. I downloaded it once, and I swear, I didn't listen to it again for about 6 months. It wasn't until I heard it on a commercial for one of those 80's CDs the I truly recognized how much this song rocked.

There have been many of a night at Boneshakers when I had to drop everything to dance to this little ditty. I can even recall a few of those times to be extremely pleasant, and a few that were extremely ackward. This song doesn't really mean that much to me, it's just a fun song I like to sing and dance to. Other than that, it belongs to no one else.
Fleetwood Mac - Sara

 Funny thing about this song. I didn't even like it that much. I mean, "Rhiannon" to me was alot better, I guess I just understood the story better. I don't know, but there was something about that nasty hurricane that blew in here a few weeks ago that just got me closer to this song. We didn't have any power, and I was writing one of my Blogs, and all of a sudden "Sara" came on.

In all the wind and the rain, lightning and thunder, I guess I just heard it for the first time. It's a really touching and beautiful song. Like everything else, it reminds me of Matt. He gave me the mega-mix of Fleetwood Mac that now invests my hard drive, and this song really captures its infection in my heart. It's just so sad sometimes, it makes me want to cry.

Ok, I'll stop showing off my HTML skills with complex tables and a masterful manipulation of text. I'm sorry, but if you can View Source anything, take a look at that table. Written by hand, thank you very much. Maybe it's not as impressive as I probably could be (and will be), but I've come along way from having Jeremy make tables for me on his computer so I could use them on mine. Now I am the master.

So, enough music for today. I really wish that iPod will be mailed already. I know it's a waiting game, but I think I'm just killing my laptop's power supply on a daily basis when I plug in like this just to listen to music. When I'm all iPod in my music habits, I suspect I will stay off this portable idiot machine more often. I can't really tell if Winamp takes up substantial power supply, it's hard to say. God knows the wireless internet sure does, so it's a good thing I've kept away from that.

In other me news, I am considering going to Las Vegas in January. I haven't decided what I want to do about that. It would be a nice vacation, but vacations are the last thing that I need to think about right now. January is so far away from now, it seems, anyways. Gambling and "free drinks", as Jennifer has pointed out, also seem to be a moot point in comparison to things now. I am thinking I may have some miraculous moment of perspective sometime soon, and I'll decide to go. It gets me out of this hell hole for a good weekend, at the very least.

John also invited me to D.C. on Inauguration Day. I would only consider the trip if John Kerry won, because I have this aversion to see President Bush shaking his "Bon Bon" on stage again with Ricky Martin. What the hell was he thinking?! In related news, there's this charming bumper sticker I pass every day that says, "The road to hell is paved with Republicans." Something to consider.

Alright, I should really stop and track down a fax machine. Wish me luck.
