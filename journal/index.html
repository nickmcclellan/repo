Welcome to my Blog Archive. This is where all the old school HTML Blogs have been stored. They are ordered earliest Blog to latest (though the earliest Blog was written during the Fall of 2001, the internet version of the journal did not appear until the summer of 2004). Please enjoy the stroll down memory lane.

Blog is a place I frequently go to write about random stuff, some of it interesting, some of it vaguely familiar to my friends, but none of it deeply personal. What I write here I intend to be honest about and in no way is ever intended to be kept from anyone. Sometimes I do make suggestions about topics that lead readers to make their own assumptions about them, but I'm asking you not to do this. My Blog is a place where people can read my musings about the week and see how it has affected my sanity. If you think I'm trying to say something specific with my Blog, ask me about it and don't make assumptions. I really hate this single aspect about Blogging, assumptions. They make an ASS out of U and ME. You can e-mail me at yoshi369@yahoo.com or IM me at basuraboi3 if you have any comments. Thanks, and enjoy my BLOG!

The Freshman Year Blog: This is where it all began. On a boring night in January 2002, my website was born. In this Blog retrospective, I point out some of the more interesting times of my first year at UGA. I came out. I met some of the most amazing people with whom I'm still friends with. And most importantly, I created this website. If you don't feel like combing through several various individual entries, then sit back, and take a look at this one. It's got everything that's worth knowing.

These entries are very personal, so read at your own risk. It recalls a time when I was very wet behind the ears, and apparently everything had to do with sex...and everybody wanted me!

The Sophomore Year Blog: After an eventful Freshman Year, I returned to UGA with a mission. I may have been a little green when it came to keeping up with my Internet Journal, but I soon learned to iron out the kinks. My second year turned out the be the most interesting. I became very close with Missy, and we had some amazing adventures. I dated a whole lot more than my first year. I broke some hearts (got my own heart broken), and even fell in love. If you'd like to get the whole (abridged) version of the gory details, make sure you take a look at this one.

Spring and Summer 2003: Embracing a "beautifulgarbage/Breaking Up The Girl" view of the world, I went for blue skies in the update for the Spring of 2003. Click here to read about the semester and summer that turned out to be exactly what I wasn't expecting.

The Junior Year Blog: At the beginning of my third year at college, I had finally found my place. I got an apartment off campus, remained close to my best friends, and fell hopelessly in love with Matt. While I continued to struggle with classes, I nonetheless worked very hard and bested some difficult courses. I went from party star to dutiful SO staying at home on a Saturday night—it was nuts. I experienced my most difficult year: I lost my car, my cell phone busted, and my computer bit the big one. I grew up a whole lot that year. Make sure you check out the year that may not have turned many heads but still managed to be my favorite.
Fall 2003: Venturing into my first semester as an upperclassman at UGA, I produced a very attractive "Stripped" theme, demonstrating the sophistication of my developing HTML skills. Click here to read the Blog about the raunchiest semester by far!
Spring and Summer 2004: Probably one of the most difficult semesters I've ever gone through, I impressively triumphed over several personal and academic obstacles. I lost the Shitmobile, grew up alot, and celebrated One Year with Matt. Plus, the Blog has never looked this good.

The Senior Year Blog: My final year at UGA was the most dark and depressing of all my years at UGA. Matt decided to leave Athens behind, and me along with it. I became very depressed and my website and Blog suffered. Despite Matt's decision, I was eventually able to soldier through it. My final semester at UGA was challenging, but ultimately a success. I got to see my favorite band in concert, reconnect to some of my best friends, and I quietly prepared what would be my final update for my website. At the end of the summer, I was able to leave Athens behind. Please read the Senior Year Blog to learn how it all came to an end.
Fall 2004: By far the worst semester I've ever had in Athens. Matt ran away, and in an attempt to keep it together, my Blog suffered from many missing entries and cryptic admissions. Antonio and my Uncle passed away...it is a semester I will never forget—and always resent.
Spring 2005: For my last Blog, I produced the most ambitious update ever. I attended my last semester at UGA, got to go to my very first solo garbage concert, and I graduated with two degrees. I found the strength to endure, despite my still stinging break-up with Matt, and at the end of the summer, I was able to run away.
