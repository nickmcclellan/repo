Noise That Keeps Me Awake

Monday, January 26th...TRIAL DAY!!!

Today's the big day. Noise ordinance trial. I hope I have money to give back to Matt and Liz. If not, I'm going to be sad...and broke.

AND I have the flu...or something flu-like. Isn't that nice. Class at 9:05 a.m. Even better. Just let everything freeze, and I'll be better.


I WON!!! Yes, the noise ordinance trial was a complete success! I don't know if the average avid-Blog reader is aware, but during Chris and Liz's party last semester, things got out of control in more than a Missy w/ boys way. A cop showed up (which isn't unexpected at my parties), and I was, for some odd reason, NOT drunk and able to deal with the situation. He basically told me that we were violating the noise ordinance and he was going to give me a ticket. Everyone at the party was bummed for me as I went to my room to look to see how much the fine would be...$125. Sucky.

I instantly wanted to fight the citation. My court date was some day in November. It was a Thursday and it was cold. I showed up and there were tons of people in the hall waiting to enter the court room. It was very interesting, I don't mean to be pretentious, but it was sociologically facinating. It was really interesting seeing these black men complain about cops only wanting money from them. I think it was alittle more complicated than that. Anyways, I was scared shitless. I didn't realize that I wouldn't have the opportunity to explain my case at this court date, I had to wait for a trial! That's really intimidating. I mean, witnesses, cross-examinations! It was very scary, I almost paid for the citation.

Something stopped me though. We have this right in the constitution to a fair trial, and I am the sort of wide-eyed idiot that believes that maybe I too have a chance at justice. Seeing this guy get sentenced for DUI, it was really neat. The judge talked all fast and I thought maybe I'd see if there really was a bias against college student in Athens. I also got two months to make the money for the ticket in the event that I decided to change my plea.

As the trial neared, I got more and more confident, more and more lazy, and then last week, I got kinda scared. If they would have let me change my plea over the phone, I would have done it. This weekend, while I was sick and bored, I got to thinking. There's no evidence against us. It's their word against ours. I regained my confidence. I called Marilia and Jeremy over last night and we built our case. Marilia said I wrote my statement well. We joked on the way over to the courthouse, I was ready for anything. Win or lose, I had my checkbook or a pocketfull of cash for a celebratory milk shake.

We walked in, sat down. Read the trial procedures sheet. Scary, I would be asking my witnesses questions instead of reading a statement...wasn't expecting that. Pressure rising. The judge called some guy to the bench. She informed in that the officer that was subpoenaed in his case did not show up, so charges were dropped against him and he was free to go. I thought to myself, "Damn, that's nice!" Wouldn't be that easy for me...

Then she called me and suddenly I thought I must be first. I came to the bench and she informed me and two other guys that were called to the bench that the officers subpoenaed in our cases did not appear in court and the charges would be dropped against us and we were free to leave. HELL FUCKIN' YEAH! It was over! We were in that room for five minutes, and it was over! Marilia, Jeremy, and I went to the Grill and Gyro Wrap to celebrate. I called some people. Matt had just sent me a "Good Luck" text message right when I called to give him the news. He was really surprised.

I was on Cloud 9 after leaving the court. It's over. Not only is this noise ordinance crap over, but classes were cancelled today and I got to legitimately skip the classes I was going to skip anyway because I was sick/dealing with legal issues. It's been a really good day for me, probably one of the best I've had in a long time. Seriously, I said something about it being the best day ever, but maybe not quite. I don't think I achieved anything today, I just got really lucky. Maybe that day of achievement will come sometime soon, maybe in May. For now, I'm going to lay down or something. I earned it.
