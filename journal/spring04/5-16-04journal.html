When she got that car, I swear, that day I felt absolutely worthless. I feel the exact opposite today. Having someone give me something so precious to them because they love me is incredible. Thursday, May 20th

Welcome to my new Dell! Yup, that's right. Here I am in the bowels of my new computer. Poor little Pro-Star will likely collect dust in the closet. It's sad, really...

I've had some issues with the Dell. For one, all that crap software and utitilies they send with it. I'm sorry, but if I'm going to connect to my wireless network, I'll do it, I don't need some Dell utility to hold my hand through the whole "ordeal." Oh, yah, and this one utility set my power preferences. I took one look at it, and--Surprise! Surprise!--it's virtually the same Windows utility when you right click on the battery icon. I think I've gotten most of the Dell crap. I would like to dump the Solution Center...might make a siege tomorrow. I'll let it sit in my Start folder--for now!

The resolution on my new computer is pretty amazing. Fonts look so pretty! The sound is better. The theme needs to be tweaked. You know how creative I am. Viewing my website through this computer has revealed to me an imperfection in the background of my current website theme. If you've noticed it, it's been fixed. Hmmm, other than that, everything is normal.

Not much going on here. I'm going to go over to Matt's tonight for a change of pace. Britcoms and Friends. Hopefully the boy has bought some food. I've done some serious snacking today. Too many Peeps (don't ask me how I got my hands on those in May).

Have you ever seen TechTV? It's an excellent channel! My favorite shows are the Screensavers and Call for Help. For some reason, Call for Help's last show is on Friday. If you have a chance, watch it. It's very helpful. Just yesterday, they told me about this very useful utility called Yahoo Pops that enables Outlook Express to access a Yahoo mail account without making you pay the $20 fee that Yahoo asks for. I haven't been able to get into Outlook Express since I was a freshman! I very much prefer this interface to the web interface that's full of ads and other nastiness. I've also found programs like IE Privacy Keeper (which regularly cleans cookies, history, and temporary Internet files) and other things. They highlight so many common utilities in the many operating systems available. For example, they showed me howto enable the Internet firewall included in Windows XP--an excellent way to protect your PC. If you're knowledgeable on all the techno-babble, you will love these programs!

Well, I wanted to make an entry on the new computer, and I guess that's done. This computer is awesome, so much nicer than the last one, and get this! BATTERY! 5 HOURS LONG! That's one upgrade I DON'T regret. Hell yes...
