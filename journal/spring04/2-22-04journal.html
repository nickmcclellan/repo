Ode to the Shitmobile

Sunday, February 22nd

The First Ode to the Shitmobile

At last, my car, my own reclining seat
A place to toss receipts next to my feet
My car, a way to walk a thousand miles
My bike, exiled to where my childhood piles

It starts, I tingle, leaving all my toys
A pause--oh no! What is that little noise?
I drive, ignore the mild transmission rumble
A turn, no fears the wheels may start to tumble

The beginning, my sister eats her Subway meal
Pressing the gas, I wake the Shitmobile.

Monday, February 23rd

The Second Ode to the Shitmobile

I never thought my car was something odd
Colored grey and bore and more on dented doors
Never fooled by citrus scent facade
A mildew musk, like nothing known before

Those Summer days, romantic walks in Hell
My handheld fan, a freon-less escape
Like demon breath upon my sweaty nape
Trapped inside this four-door prison cell

Back and forth, to work and home I go
While slowly melting engine heart below...

Tuesday, February 24th

The Third Ode to the Shitmobile

My Junior year, Odometer did grow
Ignorant to problems from below
Bubblin' up from gaskets, tanks, and pipes
Whilst I enduring petty sibling fights

At last, it came. The car did stop
Motor melting with a "Pop!"
And with steam rising in the air
Twas likely months until repair

Summer came and I was bumming rides
Spending thousands fixing my car's insides...

Wednesday, February 25th

The Fourth Ode to the Shitmobile

At last my car returned to me anew
Though it cost alot, things didn't seem askew
If only I knew...

The transmission rumbled until it stopped
And then it dropped

After double-cheese, fries and a coke
The battery broke

There was that time, on route to work
The alternator went beserk

Confused and angry, I asked for aid
Discouraged from costs that I had paid
My dad refused to assist my cause
Knowing there were too many flaws

So off I went, time bomb ticking away
Helpless to stop that fateful day...

Thursday, February 26th

The Fifth Ode to the Shitmobile

Hell hath no fury like Shitmobile scorned
Taking the first step, heeding all that was warned
Hundreds I spent to prevent its demise
Halting the problems that were sure to arise

So after it all, I went for a ride
Summer sun beaming, confident in stride
My alternator decided to take a short break
In perfect time for my little mistake

Turns out it's not healthy for black to touch red
The battery sparked and my heart filled with dread
It's inards decided to sizzle and bake
Taking more money that I'd have to make

That summer was hard, I worked overnight
My Shitmobile worked, now college in sight
Packing my things, I proceeded to go
Though more and more problems started to show...

Friday, February 27th

The Sixth Ode to the Shitmobile

My years at college, the best of my life
Though sporadicly fraught with Shitmobile strife
Through years having suffered electrical woe
Now finally arrived at my personal low

Listening to music, I saw with a fright
My CD player stop and turn out the light
I had to slow down and take caution at first
Realizing, sadly, my car had been cursed!

For months I could not go faster than thirty
Without my music flying off like a birdie
Angry and bitter, I had reached the end
Refusing to send the car back on the mend...

Saturday, February 28th

The Last Ode to the Shitmobile

Oh Shitmobile! The time has come to leave
My bitch, so tempting and dainty like Eve
You've taken me places that I've never been
Though cursing me like original sin

While comfort was never a strong suit for you
You've comforted me like I never knew
When computers and phones and other things broke
I always thought you'd be the next to choak

Certainly you've busted a hubcap or two
But you were always there when I needed you
To work, to home, to places unknown
Buckled in tight on my grey-seated throne

But now you've gone to darken another's day
While I, relinquished to peddle my bike away
I think of memories, cherished and sweet
Memories of which I'll NEVER repeat

I curse you and your smelly ass
Burn in hell, you piece of shit!
