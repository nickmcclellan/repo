Wednesday, October 16th

What was today? Was today just another one of those days that are swallowed into nothing? One of the most amazing images of oblivion I have ever found would have to be John Milton's description of Death in Paradise Lost. The idea that Death is a formless being with a large, vicious jaw is just so captivating. Just imagining this beast behind us all, stalking us, partaking in our souls when he sees fit, consuming us. We are swallowed down into his gut, lost forever...saved in the end by our Savior, the one who died for our sins.

Was that too much to take? I'm rarely that religious, but that's how I look at things. It's exciting. Was that what happened to today? Is it lost? Is it one of those days that in my final days, I'm going to wish I could live again to the fullest? I don't know. I feel that I did something. I did my laundry. I mulled over my paper topic. I had a very nice nap (the dream involved a very strange individual who was asking me if he could work for me at Target. Apparently I was visiting Target, and this guy was asking if he could work for me sometime. I told him that I had been on leave since mid-August and told him he could work for me sometime during Christmas when I came back...he was cute, I remember that, and wore an interesting blue shirt). I set up a study date with Padmini for Thursday (at Blue Sky, I'm excited about that, I've always wanted to have a study group there). What does that do for me though? Is this just a waste? Should I treat my precious time here with more respect?

I was thinking, or I have been thinking that it's these days I wish I could relive again and again. It's like in that play Our Town, the girl who dies at the end, afterlife is just a bunch of dead people sitting in a cemetery talking about anything but life. They are aloud to relive any day they wish, but most choose not to. The girl, recently dead, questions the "veteran" dead people, and decides to relive the most important days of her life. Days like when her husband proposed to her, the last time she saw her mother. It was in these times she found that though she (knowing she was reliving the moment and realizing how valuable it was) realized that the people around her, the people she loved, their view of the day had not changed. They acted and reacted in the same way they did the first time it happened. It was horrible torture for her to see her mother treating the time in such a normal routine. She eventually returns to the cemetery, hurt and saddened, sitting their for eternity, trying to forget her life.

Is that what will happen to me? Will I come back to this day, some random day, just to relive it, to know what it felt to be normal? I don't know, but I think about these things.

Ok, I hope you enjoyed that. I was trying to be existential. Did it work?

I'm writing my paper, that's good right? Of course it is, I don't know why I would ask you anyways. I'm sitting here listening to "Coffee and T.V." This song reminds me of junior year. It reminds me of first semester junior year, a most boring time. I had started driving to school and I still worked at Kroger, but I was about to quit. I had to drive my sister to school, and during the times I didn't listen to garbage, I had in either Wierd Al (don't ask), the Buffy soundtrack, or this song.

There is something about "Coffee and T.V." Maybe it's the video, maybe it's just the laid back catchiness, I dunno. Someone asked me once what song I thought just summed up what LIFE was for me. I couldn't say some garbage song, because they are so varied. I would definitely say that garbage albums are life. There are times of optimism, times of depression, and times when you just want to scream "FUCK OFF" to everyone and "Rip it all to shreads and LET IT GO!" But one song? What song would I choose? "Coffee and T.V. is kinda long, and it just rants on and on at the end "We can start over again..." That's positive right? You can do that? Of course, not on your death bed, but you know what I mean. Life to me is just dodging distractions, keeping your eye on the big picture, and not getting lost along the way. Having that friendly milk carton pick you out of that dirty gutter and take you back to the people you love :-)

I wanted to spill something on here on my whole "savior" motif in some of my poems. I really think that has all changed for me. For the longest time, I had ingrained in my head that there was this one person out there that's supposed to do it for me. I don't believe that anymore. I feel like, kinda what I was saying in "The Garfield Monologue", that there is no savior. He's inside all of us. We are the ones that save ourselves. We rise above to notion that one man or woman has inside of them everything that is lacking in us. Of course, it's so perfect to find that one guy that you just fall into his arms and you feel whole and loved, but can you tell me when that has happened before? I mean, sure, April and Matt, but come on! They aren't human! I swear, everyone knows April is from Mars, or maybe some dark hole somewhere. I think that if you expect one guy to make things perfect for you, you are setting yourself up for disappointment. You are your only savior, and if you can make it through to the end, you're really amazing. If you end up hand and hand with another hopeless runaway, then you've done it. You've found eachother.
